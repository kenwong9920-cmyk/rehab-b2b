Selecting the right electric wheelchair for your distribution business or healthcare facility
requires careful evaluation of technical specifications, certifications, and market requirements.
Here's what you need to know.

## Key Specifications to Evaluate

### 1. Motor & Battery System

The motor and battery are the heart of any electric wheelchair. For B2B buyers, consider:

- **Motor Power**: 250W dual motors are standard for mid-range models. Higher wattage (350W+) is needed for heavier users
  or hilly terrain.
- **Battery Type**: Lithium batteries are becoming the industry standard — lighter, longer-lasting, and more reliable
  than traditional lead-acid batteries.
- **Range**: Most standard models offer 15-20km per charge. Consider what range your target customers will need.

### 2. Frame & Weight

- **Material**: Aluminum alloy offers the best balance of strength and weight. Steel frames are cheaper but heavier.
- **Weight**: A lightweight chair (under 20kg without battery) is easier to transport and more appealing to end users.
- **Foldability**: Quick-fold mechanisms are a major selling point for retail markets.

### 3. Comfort & Adjustability

Look for:
- Adjustable armrests and footrests
- Multiple seat width options
- Ergonomic joystick positioning
- Breathable seat and backrest materials

## Certifications Matter

For different markets, you'll need different certifications:

| Market | Required Certifications |
|--------|------------------------|
| European Union | CE Mark, ISO 13485 |
| United States | FDA Registration, ISO 13485 |
| United Kingdom | UKCA Mark |
| Australia | TGA Registration |
| Canada | MDL License |

**Always verify that your manufacturer has current, valid certifications** before placing orders.
Ask for certificate copies and verify them if possible.

## OEM Customization: What's Possible

As a B2B buyer, you can differentiate your product line through OEM customization:

1. **Frame Colors** — Any RAL color, including two-tone options
2. **Branding** — Your logo on the frame, seat, joystick, and packaging
3. **Battery Specs** — Upgrade capacity for extended range
4. **Controller Settings** — Adjust speed, acceleration, and braking profiles
5. **Packaging** — Custom branded boxes and multilingual manuals

## MOQ and Pricing Considerations

Typical MOQs for OEM electric wheelchairs range from **10 to 50 units** depending on customization level.
Per-unit pricing typically decreases by 15-25% when ordering container quantities (50+ units).

## Questions to Ask Your Manufacturer

Before placing an order, ask:

- Can you provide samples for evaluation?
- What is your typical production lead time?
- Do you offer after-sales support and spare parts?
- What quality control processes do you follow?
- Can you provide references from existing B2B clients?
