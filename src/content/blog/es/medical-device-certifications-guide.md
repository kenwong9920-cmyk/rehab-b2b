Navigating medical device certifications is one of the biggest challenges for B2B importers
of rehabilitation equipment. This guide breaks down the three most important certifications.

## CE Marking (European Union)

**What it is**: The CE mark indicates that a product complies with EU health, safety,
and environmental requirements.

**Who needs it**: Any medical device sold in the European Economic Area (EEA).

**Key points for B2B buyers**:
- CE marking is **mandatory** for EU market access
- It's a **self-declaration** for Class I devices, but requires a Notified Body for higher classes
- Rehabilitation equipment typically falls under Class I or Class IIa
- Always request the **Declaration of Conformity** document from your manufacturer

## FDA Registration (United States)

**What it is**: FDA registration means a medical device establishment and its devices
are listed with the US Food and Drug Administration.

**Key points for B2B buyers**:
- FDA **registration** is not the same as FDA **approval** (which is only for Class III devices)
- Most rehabilitation equipment is Class I or Class II — requiring registration and listing
- FDA requires annual renewal of establishment registration
- Verify your manufacturer's FDA registration at the [FDA Establishment Registration database](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfRL/rl.cfm)

## ISO 13485 (Global)

**What it is**: An international standard for Quality Management Systems specific to medical devices.

**Key points for B2B buyers**:
- ISO 13485 demonstrates a **systematic approach to quality management**
- It covers design, production, storage, distribution, and servicing
- Often required alongside CE or FDA for regulatory compliance
- Certification is issued by accredited bodies (e.g., SGS, TÜV, BSI)

## Comparison Table

| Aspect | CE Mark | FDA Registration | ISO 13485 |
|--------|---------|------------------|-----------|
| Scope | EU Market | US Market | Global |
| Focus | Product Safety | Public Health | Quality System |
| Validity | Ongoing | Annual Renewal | 3 Years |
| Testing Required | Yes | Depends on Class | System Audit |

## What This Means for Your Import Business

1. **For EU markets**: Ensure CE + ISO 13485
2. **For US markets**: Ensure FDA Registration + ISO 13485
3. **For other markets**: CE + ISO 13485 is widely accepted, but check local regulations

## Verify Before You Buy

Always request:
- Valid certificate copies (check expiration dates!)
- Declaration of Conformity (for CE)
- FDA Owner/Operator Number (for FDA)
- ISO 13485 certificate with scope of registration
