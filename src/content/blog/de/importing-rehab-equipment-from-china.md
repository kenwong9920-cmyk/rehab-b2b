Importing rehabilitation equipment from China can be highly profitable if done correctly.
Here are ten practical tips based on our experience working with B2B buyers worldwide.

## 1. Verify Certifications Early

Before sending a deposit, request **current copies** of:
- CE Certificate and Declaration of Conformity
- FDA Registration number
- ISO 13485 certificate (check expiry date and scope)
- Any product-specific test reports (EN 12182, EN 12183, etc.)

## 2. Request Factory Audit Reports

A factory audit (or schedule your own visit) reveals:
- Actual production capacity vs. claimed capacity
- Quality control processes in practice
- Working conditions and workforce stability
- Equipment maintenance and calibration records

## 3. Start with a Sample Order

Never go straight to container orders. Sample orders let you:
- Evaluate product quality firsthand
- Test market acceptance with your customers
- Identify any documentation or packaging issues

## 4. Clarify Incoterms

Make sure your contract specifies:
- **FOB** (Free on Board): Manufacturer handles everything until goods are on the vessel
- **CIF** (Cost, Insurance, Freight): Manufacturer covers shipping to your port
- **DDP** (Delivered Duty Paid): Manufacturer handles everything to your door

## 5. Understand Packaging Requirements

Specify exactly what you need:
- Individual box packaging vs. bulk packaging
- Pallet specifications for your warehouse
- Multilingual manuals (which languages?)
- Assembly instructions format (printed, QR code, video)

## 6. Plan for Logistics and Lead Times

Typical lead times for rehabilitation equipment:
- Standard products: 15-25 days
- OEM customized: 30-45 days
- Sea freight to Europe: 30-40 days
- Sea freight to US West Coast: 15-20 days
- Sea freight to Southeast Asia: 7-15 days

## 7. Quality Control Before Shipment

Insist on:
- Pre-shipment inspection (AQL sampling)
- Photos and videos of finished goods before payment
- Third-party inspection if you can't visit personally

## 8. Build a Relationship, Not Just a Transaction

The best B2B partnerships come from:
- Regular communication (WeChat/WhatsApp)
- Annual factory visits
- Clear, documented specifications
- Fair negotiation that leaves room for quality

## 9. Protect Your IP

For OEM products:
- Sign an NDA before sharing detailed designs
- Register your brand/trademark in China
- Work with manufacturers who have a track record of respecting IP

## 10. Plan for After-Sales Service

Discuss before ordering:
- Spare parts availability and pricing
- Warranty terms (typically 1-2 years)
- Technical support channels
- Return/repair procedures
